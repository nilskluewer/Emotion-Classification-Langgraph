Synthesized Report

I. Reason for Assessment (Purpose of Analysis)

This analysis aims to understand the constructed emotional experiences of an anonymous online forum user based on their comments on the Der Standard website during May 2019, applying the theory of constructed emotions.

II. Identifying Information

  • Name/Username: Anonymized Online Forum User
  • Source of Data: Der Standard online comment section (01.05.2019 - 31.05.2019)

III. Data Sources and Methods:

This analysis utilizes textual data from the user's comments. The methodology involves analyzing the language used, considering the context of the articles commented on, and applying the principles of constructed emotions, focusing on core affect, cognitive appraisals, conceptual knowledge, and sociocultural influences. This approach acknowledges the limitations of text-based analysis and avoids definitive claims about the user's subjective emotional experience. Instead, it focuses on observable patterns and potential interpretations based on the available data.

IV. Behavioral Observations:

  • Core Affect: The user predominantly exhibits negative valence, particularly in politically charged discussions, coupled with varying levels of arousal. High arousal is evident in strongly worded comments and active engagement in debates, while lower arousal is observed in more neutral comments on less personally relevant topics.
  • Emotional Language: The user employs strong, emotionally charged language, including terms like "Widerliches Schmierentheater," "Hetzreden," and "Faktenverdrehung," indicating negative affect and high arousal related to political figures and events. Sarcasm and wit are also employed, potentially as a form of emotional regulation.
  • Interaction Patterns: The user actively engages in discussions, often responding directly to other commenters, particularly on political topics. This suggests a desire to express opinions and participate in public discourse.

V. Contextual and Cultural Considerations:

The analysis considers the Austrian socio-political climate of May 2019, marked by the Ibiza affair and subsequent political turmoil. This context likely influenced the user's emotional expressions, particularly the strong negative affect towards certain political figures and parties. The user's engagement in political discussions reflects a cultural norm of public discourse and participation.

VI. Interpretation and Conceptualization:

The user's emotional expressions appear to be constructed through the interplay of negative core affect, critical cognitive appraisals of political figures as dishonest and manipulative, and a conceptual understanding of right-wing populism. The socio-political context amplifies these constructed emotions, leading to strong expressions of anger, disgust, and contempt. The use of humor and sarcasm may represent a culturally influenced strategy for regulating emotional expression.

VII. Limitations:

This analysis is based solely on text data and cannot definitively determine the user's subjective emotional experience. It offers potential interpretations based on observable patterns and the theory of constructed emotions, acknowledging the inherent limitations of inferring emotions from online communication.

VIII. Report Findings:

This analysis suggests that the user experienced predominantly negative affect during May 2019, particularly in response to the Austrian political climate. The user's comments reveal a critical stance towards certain political figures and parties, expressed through strong emotional language and active engagement in online debates. The Ibiza affair and the surrounding political context likely played a significant role in shaping the user's emotional responses. While positive affect is observed in non-political discussions, the dominant emotional theme revolves around negative valence and varying levels of arousal related to political events. The user's emotional expressions appear to be constructed through a complex interplay of core affect, cognitive appraisals, conceptual knowledge, and sociocultural influences. The use of humor and sarcasm suggests a potential strategy for emotional regulation within the context of online communication. This analysis provides valuable insights into the user's constructed emotional experiences during a specific period of time, while acknowledging the limitations of text-based analysis and the complexity of human emotions.
